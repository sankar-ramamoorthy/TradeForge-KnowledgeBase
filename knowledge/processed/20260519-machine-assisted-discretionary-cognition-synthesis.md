# Machine-Assisted Discretionary Cognition Synthesis

## Processing Result

The raw brainstorm established a roadmap-level strategic shift: TradeForge should evolve beyond foundational AI assistance into a machine-assisted discretionary cognition operating system.

This does not change the core invariant that humans retain decision sovereignty. It clarifies what future machine assistance is for: attention allocation, evidence accumulation, contextual interpretation, cognitive replay, behavioral auditability, and long-horizon process improvement.

## Stabilized Direction

TradeForge is not evolving toward:

- signal generation
- autonomous trading
- black-box scoring
- RL capital allocation
- predictive execution automation

TradeForge is evolving toward:

```text
a replayable discretionary cognition operating system
```

Machine systems may surface observations, candidates, evidence, conflicts, anomalies, historical comparisons, and behavioral patterns. Operators remain responsible for judgment, risk acceptance, lifecycle commitment, approval, execution decisions, and accountability.

## Core Architectural Distinction

The brainstorm stabilizes a distinction between advisory space and commitment space.

Advisory space contains:

```text
Observation
Signal
Context
Interpretation
Candidate
```

Commitment space contains:

```text
Trade Idea
Thesis
Plan
Approval
Execution
Position
Review
```

Automated systems may generate advisory artifacts. They must not directly create operator-owned lifecycle commitments or transition workflow state.

## Canonical Implications

This synthesis reinforces existing canonical invariants rather than replacing them:

- Human Decision Sovereignty remains non-negotiable.
- AI remains advisory only.
- Event Ledger truth remains canonical.
- Replayability remains foundational.
- Derived, inferred, and AI-generated interpretations must remain distinguishable from facts.
- Review and reflection remain first-class workflows.
