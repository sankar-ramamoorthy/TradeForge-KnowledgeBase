# Screen 3 — Plan Review Workspace

This is the:

```text id="pr1"
pre-execution control gate
```

This may ultimately become the most important screen in the entire system.

Why?

Because this is where:

```text id="pr2"
an idea becomes a committed risk decision
```

This is the transition from:

```text id="pr3"
thinking
```

to:

```text id="pr4"
intentional exposure
```

And that boundary is where most discretionary traders fail.

---

# Philosophical Purpose

Traditional trading systems optimize for:

```text id="pr5"
speed of execution
```

TradeForge should optimize for:

```text id="pr6"
quality of commitment
```

This screen should psychologically slow the trader down.

Not annoyingly.

But intentionally.

The user should feel:

```text id="pr7"
“I am authorizing risk.”
```

NOT:

```text id="pr8"
“I am clicking Buy.”
```

---

# Core Purpose Of The Screen

The screen answers:

```text id="pr9"
Should this opportunity
be allowed to become a live position?
```

---

# This Is NOT

## NOT A Brokerage Ticket

No giant flashing BUY/SELL controls.

No “quick trade.”

No dopamine.

---

# This IS

```text id="pr10"
a structured decision authorization workspace
```

Like:

* aviation preflight
* surgical checklist
* mission authorization
* institutional risk review

---

# Swing Trader Reality

A discretionary swing trader usually fails due to:

* oversized risk
* emotional chasing
* poor timing
* regime mismatch
* thesis inconsistency
* weak invalidation logic
* ignoring context
* violating discipline

This screen exists to intercept those failures BEFORE exposure occurs.

---
