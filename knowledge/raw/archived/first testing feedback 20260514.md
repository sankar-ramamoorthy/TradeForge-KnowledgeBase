# TradeForge M10/M10A Operational Walkthrough Feedback

## Context

This feedback captures observations from executing the current TradeForge operational workflow demo using a realistic discretionary swing-trade scenario (SMH semiconductor momentum continuation setup).

The purpose of this feedback is not architectural criticism but operational evaluation from the perspective of an actual discretionary trader attempting to use the system naturally.

---

# Overall Observation

TradeForge is beginning to transition from:

* workflow/lifecycle demonstration software

toward:

* structured discretionary cognition infrastructure.

The architectural direction is increasingly visible during use.

The strongest emerging qualities are:

* lifecycle intentionality
* separation between intent and execution
* replay-oriented cognition
* structured reasoning capture
* explicit risk authorization
* non-authoritative advisory overlays

The system increasingly feels philosophically different from traditional trading software.

---

# Strong Operational Concepts Observed

## 1. Intentional Risk Authorization

The “What risk is being intentionally authorized?” framing was operationally meaningful.

The workflow successfully separates:

* opportunity recognition
* thesis development
* execution planning
* risk authorization
* execution reality

This creates a more disciplined discretionary cognition model than traditional broker-oriented workflows.

The distinction between:

```text
intent
≠
execution
```

was consistently reinforced throughout the workflow and felt architecturally strong.

---

## 2. Structured Cognition Capture

The introduction of structured thesis and plan authoring significantly changed the operational feel of the system.

The separation of:

* thesis narrative
* catalysts
* assumptions
* invalidation conditions
* execution assumptions
* conviction
* playbook alignment

felt much more meaningful than generic trading journal notes.

This appears to be the beginning of true replayable discretionary cognition.

---

## 3. Advisory Model Feels Correct

The advisory system behaved appropriately as:

```text
non-authoritative cognitive guidance
```

rather than workflow enforcement.

The system surfaced areas for reconsideration without blocking operator sovereignty.

This aligned well with the Human Decision Sovereignty philosophy.

---

## 4. Canonical vs Derived vs Inferred Separation

The distinction between:

* canonical truth
* derived operational state
* inferred/advisory interpretation

became increasingly understandable through usage.

This separation feels architecturally coherent and operationally valuable.

---

# Important Operational Gaps Discovered

## 1. Missing Revision Workflow

The workflow currently supports progression more strongly than iterative reasoning refinement.

Example:

The system surfaces advisory items requiring review, but there is no obvious operational mechanism to:

* revise thesis
* revise assumptions
* revise invalidation logic
* revise plan details
* return to prior cognitive stages

This creates friction once cognition authoring becomes richer.

The workflow currently feels:

```text
forward-progress optimized
```

rather than:

```text
iterative discretionary reasoning optimized
```

A revision-oriented cognition loop appears necessary.

---

## 2. Authorized Intent vs Executed Position Compression

A major operational issue emerged around trade execution timing.

In real discretionary swing trading, approval often means:

```text
"If conditions occur, I am prepared to execute."
```

—not:

```text
"I have already entered the trade."
```

Example from the SMH walkthrough:

The plan explicitly required:

* daily close above 585
* rising volume
* continued semiconductor breadth participation

Operationally, the trade should remain in a persistent:

```text
authorized but unexecuted
```

state until trigger conditions are satisfied.

The current MVP flow compresses:

```text
approval
→ execution
→ position
```

too aggressively for realistic discretionary workflows.

This appears to reveal a missing lifecycle concept such as:

* Awaiting Trigger
* Authorized Watch State
* Conditional Execution State
* Armed Opportunity

This distinction feels architecturally important.

---

## 3. Data Entry / Cognition UX Ergonomics

The current cognition input areas felt overly constrained for the level of discretionary reasoning being captured.

The small textarea-style inputs psychologically resemble:

* CRUD forms
* configuration panels
* developer/admin tooling

rather than:

* operational cognition environments
* reflective decision modeling
* structured discretionary journaling

This became especially noticeable during:

* thesis narrative composition
* invalidation modeling
* execution assumptions
* structured plan authoring

The workflow increasingly demands:

```text
thinking space
```

rather than compact form completion.

The current UX appears optimized for:

```text
workflow progression
```

more than:

```text
deep cognition composition
```

---

# Architectural Realization

A major realization from the walkthrough is that the roadmap currently contains strong infrastructure for:

* workflow authority
* replayability
* lifecycle continuity
* workspace cognition
* operational navigation

However, the system is now approaching the need for a dedicated milestone around:

```text
structured cognition authoring
```

The current M10 workflow exposed the absence of a formal milestone for:

* rich thesis authoring
* structured plan composition
* replayable operator reasoning
* iterative cognition refinement
* structured review reflection
* scenario branch evolution

This led to the proposed M10A milestone concept.

---

# Overall Operational Impression

TradeForge increasingly feels less like:

* a trading dashboard
* a broker frontend
* a workflow demo

and more like:

```text
a replayable discretionary cognition system
```

The most interesting aspect of the walkthrough was not market data or workflow transitions, but the realization that the system is beginning to model:

```text
human reasoning under uncertainty
```

as a first-class operational artifact.
