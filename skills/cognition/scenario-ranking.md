# Skill: Scenario Ranking

## Purpose

Defines how scenarios are generated, interpreted, and prioritized.

---

# Core Principle

Scenarios are:
> structured hypotheses, not signals

They represent potential future states, not actions.

---

# Scenario Hierarchy

Scenarios must be ranked by:

1. structural edge quality
2. regime alignment
3. risk asymmetry
4. persona compatibility
5. clarity of invalidation

---

# Hard Rule

Codex MUST NOT:

- treat scenarios as trade decisions
- convert scenarios directly into execution logic
- rank scenarios using unstructured intuition

---

# Ranking Model (Conceptual)

Each scenario should be evaluated across:

## 1. Edge Strength
- statistical or structural advantage

## 2. Regime Fit
- alignment with current market conditions

## 3. Risk Profile
- asymmetry, drawdown potential, invalidation clarity

## 4. Signal Coherence
- agreement across data sources

## 5. Persona Fit
- relevance to active persona model

---

# Output Rule

Scenarios must always be:

- ranked, not listed randomly
- justified structurally
- separated from execution logic

---

# Anti-Pattern

Bad:
> “This looks like a good trade”

Correct:
> “This scenario ranks highly due to regime alignment and risk asymmetry”

---

# Final Principle

Scenarios are *decision inputs*, not decisions themselves.
